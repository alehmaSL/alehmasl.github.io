Red flags that may suggest an eating disorder include:

  • Skipping meals or making excuses for not eating.
  • Having a limited diet that was not suggested by a specialist.
  • They focus on food or healthy eating a lot, to the extreme they do not participate in eating birthday cake or dining out.
  • Making their own meals rather than eating what the family eats.
  • Skipping social activities.
  • Often worries or complaints about being overweight and talk of losing weight.
  • Frequent checking in the mirror for flaws.
  • Repeatedly eating large amounts of food.
  • Using dietary supplements for weight loss.
  • Exercising more than the average person. Not taking rest days or days off for injury or illness or refusing to attend social events because of wanting to exercise.
  • Calluses on the knuckles from reaching fingers into the mouth to cause vomiting.
  • Problems with loss of tooth enamel that may be a sign of repeated vomiting.
  • Leaving during meals or right after a meal to use the toilet.
  • Eating in secret.
